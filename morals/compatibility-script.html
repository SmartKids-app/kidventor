Act 1: The System Overload

THE COMPATIBILITY
SCRIPT

Tribalism 0.0 • Ego 0.0 • Partnership 1.0
SCENE START: A modest apartment in a bustling Nigerian suburb. One year into a marriage that the world said "wouldn't compile." OBALADE (Yoruba) and ADIZA (Hausa-Fulani) are looking through job boards.
ADIZA
(Laughing) My brother sent another WhatsApp broadcast about "cultural differences." He still hasn't forgiven your sister for that argument about Jollof rice back in uni.
OBALADE
(Closing his laptop) Let them run their old-school protocols, Adi. Our system is built on a different code. No bias, no rush. Just us. We have the genotypes, we have the love—we just need the "server" to host our dreams.
ADIZA
Exactly. No babies until the bank account can handle the "subscription fees" of life. It’s gradual.
TIME JUMP: 4 months later. ADIZA has secured a job as an Accountant/Store Manager. OBALADE is freelancing in Computer Engineering while helping his mother at the market.
LOCATION: THE LARGE STORE - MORNING. ADIZA is in her office. KAZIM (the driver) stands awkwardly at the door.
KAZIM
Madam, I’m sorry. The bank called. I start today. Here is my resignation letter.
ADIZA
(Stunned) Kazim? Today? I have six female staff here who have never touched a steering wheel! We have heavy supplies to move. You didn't give me a "prior notice" alert!
KAZIM exits. ADIZA looks at the keys. She stands up, zips up her company uniform jacket, and puts on the face cap. She walks into the warehouse.
TINUKE (Staff)
(Giggling) Ah! Madam Manager! You want to supply yourself? You actually look... sexy in that driver uniform o!
ADIZA
(Serious but smiling) Tinuke, if this were my personal business, would I sit and watch the system crash? Customers don't care about excuses; they care about delivery. Load the bigger bus. I’m driving.
EXTERIOR: THE ROAD SIDE - MOMENTS LATER. ADIZA is maneuvering the large bus through traffic. She spots OBALADE standing by the road, sweating, looking for a bike. She honks and pulls over.
OBALADE
(Eyes wide, then bursting into laughter) Adi?! What is this? Why are you behind the wheel of this beast?
ADIZA
(Speaking in Pidgin) Na so e be, my love! Kazim resigned this morning for a bank job. I can't let the supply chain break. Enter inside! Where are you going?
OBALADE
(Climbing in, still laughing) I was heading to the market for computer accessories. But wait... move over. If we’re doing this, we’re doing it together. Let me drive you. I can help with the heavy lifting today.
ADIZA
But your repairs? Your clients?
OBALADE
(Smiling) I’ll deliver them by the weekend. Today, my Manager needs a Driver. Let's move!

The KidVentor Logic

Zero-Bias Architecture

Adiza and Obalade show us that **Partnership > Tradition**.

1. **Resilience:** Adiza didn't wait for a hero; she became the solution.
2. **Support System:** Obalade didn't let "husband ego" stop him from assisting his wife in her work.
