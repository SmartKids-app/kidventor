Virtue 01: Respect — Reading Time: 4 Mins

The King and the
Wise Old Man

In the ancient Kingdom of Benin, there once lived a young King who believed that because he wore the crown, his logic was flawless.

The young King often ignored the advice of the village elders. "Their systems are outdated," he would argue. "The world is moving at the speed of light, and their slow ways are a bug in our progress."

He decided to build a massive bridge across the great river using only his own blueprints. He refused to consult the master builders who had studied the river’s currents for eighty years. "I have the best tools," he said. "I don't need their stories."

"A young inventor has the speed of a gazelle, but an elder has the map of the entire forest."

🌍

When the heavy rains arrived, the river rose with a fury the King hadn't calculated. His bridge, though beautiful, lacked the deep foundations needed for the river's shifting bed. Within hours, it collapsed into the water.

An old man, leaning on a staff carved from Iroko wood, walked to the shore. He looked at the wreckage and then at the King. "My King," he said softly, "The river has a memory. If you had listened to our songs, we would have told you where the water breathes."

The King realized his pride was his greatest failure. He spent the next month sitting in the dust with the elders, listening to stories of floods, soil, and the wind. When they rebuilt the bridge together, it wasn't just made of stone—it was built on wisdom. That bridge stood for a hundred years.

The Lesson

Respecting elders isn't just about manners; it's about data processing. In tech, we stand on the shoulders of giants. To build the future, you must first archive the lessons of the past.
