Virtue: Respect

The King and the Wise Old Man

Reading Time: 4 mins • Category: African Folklore

In the ancient Kingdom of Benin, there once lived a young King who believed that because he wore the crown, he knew everything.

The young King often ignored the advice of the village elders. "They are too slow," he would say. "The world is changing, and their old ways are useless now." He decided to build a massive bridge across the great river using only his own ideas, refusing to listen to the old builders who had lived by the water for eighty years.

"A young man can have the speed of a gazelle, but an old man has the map of the forest."

When the first rains came, the King's bridge collapsed. The people were stranded. An old man, leaning on his staff, walked to the King and said quietly, "My King, the river has a memory. If you had asked us, we would have told you where the water breathes."

The King realized his pride had blinded him. He spent the next month sitting with the elders, learning about the soil, the wind, and the history of the land. When they rebuilt the bridge together, it stood for a hundred years.

💡 The Lesson Learned

Respecting elders isn't just about being polite; it's about valuing experience. In technology, we stand on the shoulders of those who built the first computers. To be a great coder or leader, you must first learn to listen.
